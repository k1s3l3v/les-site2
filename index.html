Our Heritage

By Baccarat

Masters of Light

Crafted by those who speak the language of beauty to capture the island’s ethereal light. Sublime crystalline structures pay homage to the Maldives and paint the air in a thousand hues, creating a new standard as only Baccarat can.

Founded in 1764 by French King Louis XV

A symbol of elegance, a celebration of light. 

Baccarat’s legacy will unfold a new chapter in the Maldives. The iconic Juvisy service, which has adorned the ceremonial tables of the Elysée Palace since 1899, will find a new home, casting its light upon the pristine beauty of the Maldivian landscape, creating an oasis of opulence within the realm of SH Hotels & Resorts.

HKS Architects is a global architecture and design firm with a rich history and a significant presence in the field. Founded in 1939 by Harwood K. Smith, the firm has expanded its reach over the decades and has become one of the largest and most influential architectural practices worldwide. HKS is known for its diverse portfolio of projects and its commitment to sustainability, innovation, and creating spaces that positively impact communities.

1508 London is a luxury design studio based in London, known for creating high-end interiors and architectural designs. The name “1508” originates from the studio’s founding year, highlighting its deep roots and experience in the design industry, with recognition for bespoke and innovative design solutions. Its portfolio covers prestigious clients and brands, showcasing a sophisticated design aesthetic that combines modern luxury with timeless elegance.

SH Hotels & Resorts, an affiliate of global private investment firm Starwood Capital Group, is a hotel brand management company that operates 1 Hotels, Baccarat Hotels, Treehouse Hotels, and SH Collection properties. Combining confident taste and creativity, frequently giving artists carte blanche, such as Francois Houtin, Cyprien Chabert or Alix Waline,to grace the walls of their projects. A timeless approach that has earned them international recognition.
